THE LEGEND OF OAKEN FYRE

By: Harper

A long time ago in a magical world between the Sea of Mor and Koad Grove, there was a stone that marked the greatest war of OAKEN FYRE and Saille Willow pixies. The name of the stone was The Stone of War!

Beside the stone was a forest of Coll Hazel trees. Inside the trees were the mythical OAKAN FYRE FAIRIES. The Oaken Fyre were the fairies who defeated the Saille Willow pixies. Ever since then, the pixies were trapped in the stone and wanted revenge.

One day the leader of Oaken Fyre was flying to the Stone of War to see if the pixies escaped. When she got there, she saw a little girl pulling up the stone. The leader shouted “NOOOO!” But it was to late. The girl pulled up the stone and the pixies were released.

The leader went back to the village of Oaken Fyre shouting “THE PIXIES ARE BACK! GET YOUR ARROWS OUT AND FIGHT!!!” A minute later, the whole village had arrows and ready to fight. Then a pixie came. Then another. And another. And then a whole army of pixies!

Oaken Fyre used arrows to defend themselves. The leader remembered that last time they defeated the pixies, they used the magical Key of Protection. If you use the key on someone, they will be stuck in the stone. They can only escape if someone lifts it up.(someone who is not trapped in the stone.)

Some fairies helped the leader try to find the key. They searched the trees, secret rooms, underground roots, and secret passageways.

They were about to give up, but then, a little girl came into the hole of the Coll Hazel tree. When she came in, the leader immediately recognized the Key of Protection the girl was holding. The leader said”if you give me the key, I will help you get back to your world.”

The girl saw the fairies fighting desperately and agreed to give the key, and gave it to them. The leader took the key and her bow and used the bow to shoot the key.

A minute later, the pixies disappeared and so did the key, but the rock appeared again.

Two fairies carried a sign that read DO NOT TOUCH and put it beside the stone.

That night, they had a feast and the girl was also there. they danced, and played games, and had fun until three o’clock in the morning.

After two days, the girl left

This is: THE LEGEND OF OAKEN FYRE
